History Of Computer

The Main Stages Or Eras Of Computer Development Are
1) Mechanical Calculating Devices

The simple mechanical devices that can be used for calculations.
2) Mechanical Calculators With Storage

Mechanical calculators with storage capabilities which could perform arithmetic operations and also retain and recall information for more complex calculations
3) Electro-Mechanical Devices

Electro-Mechanical Devices contain electrical parts and mechanical parts as well. It helps to automate the some functions of the machine. Mark I is an example of elctro- mechanical computer.
4) Electronic Devices

Electronic devices are machines or components that use electrical energy to perform a variety of functions. These devices can process information and perform other tasks using electronic circuits, transistors, and other components.

The Example Of Mechanical Calculators Are
Abacus

Abacus is considered as the first calculating device.It was used by chinese, Russian and Europeans in the past.
Napier's Bone

A Scottish Mathematician John Nepier developed the Nepier's Bone in 1617 AD. It has 11 rods with numbers form 0 to 9 in each of them.
Slide Rule

An English mathematician William Oughtred developed Slide Rule in 1620 AD. It is a mechanical analog calculator.
Pascaline

A French mathematician philosopher Blaise Pascal invented Pascaline in 1642 AD.
Difference Engine And Analytical Engine

An English Mathmematician, Charles Babbage invented Difference Engine in 1822 AD. It was used to generate tabies and slove algebraic problems.

The Example Of Electro-Mechanical Devices
Mark-I

Mark-I was developed by Howard Aiken with the assistance of IBM in between 1937 to 1944 AD. It was the first electro-mechanical computer.
ABC

ABC (Atanasoft Berry Computer) it is the first digital computer developed by Dr.John Atanasoff in 1937 to 1942 AD.

The Example Of Electronic Devices
ENIAC

ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Calculator) is developed by John Willium Mauchly and J.P.Eckert at Cambridge University in 1949 AD. It was the first general purpose computer.
EDVAC

EDVAC (Electronic Discrete Variable Automatic Computer) is one of the first large electronic mainframe computers that utilized the binary system instead of decimal system. It was developed by John William Mauchly, John Presper Ecket in 1952 AD.
EDSAC

EDSAC (Electronic Delay Storage Automatic Calculator) was the first stored program electronic computer developed by Professor Maurice Wilkes and his team in 1949 AD.
UNIVAC

UNIVAC (Universal Automatic Computer) was the first general purpose electronic digital computer for commercial users. It was developed by John William Mauchly, John Presper Eckert in 1951 AD.

Cyber Ethics

Introduction To Cyber Ethics
Cyber Ethics refers to the moral principles and guidelines that govern the use of computers and the internet.
Just like how there are rules in the real world, there are rules online to ensure everyone's safety and privacy.

Importance Of Cyber Ethics
Respect For Others

Treat others online the way you would like to be treated.
Privacy

Everyone has a right to keep their personal information private.
Safety

By following ethical guidelines, we can protect ourselves and others from cyber threats like hacking, cyberbullying, and scams.

Key Principles Of Cyber Ethics
Honesty

Do not lie, steal, or cheat online. This includes copying someone's work without permission

Respect for Privacy

Do not share other people's personal information or invade their privacy.
Responsibility

Be responsible for your actions online. Think before you post or share anything.
Integrity

Stay true to your values and principles even when no one is watching.

Common Cyber Ethics Violations
Cyberbullying

Harassing or bullying someone online.
Hacking

Accessing someone else's computer or online accounts without permission.
Plagiarism

Copying someone else's work and claiming it as your own.
Phishing

Sending fake emails or messages to steal personal information.

How to Practice Good Cyber Ethics
Strong Passwords

Use strong, unique passwords to protect your accounts.
Think Before You Post

Once something is online, it's hard to remove it. Always think about the impact of your actions.
Be A Responsible Digital Citizen

Respect others opinions, avoid spreading false information, and report unethical behavior.
Educate Yourself

Stay informed about the latest online threats and how to stay safe.

Cyber Ethics and the Law
Many unethical online behaviors are illegal, like hacking and cyberbullying. Following cyber ethics not only helps you be a good person online but also keeps you on the right side of the law.

Role of Parents and Teachers
Guidance

They can guide you in understanding the importance of cyber ethics.
Supervision

They help monitor online activities to ensure safe and ethical use of the internet.
Education

They provide the necessary knowledge to navigate the internet responsibly.

Conclusion
Cyber Ethics is essential for creating a safe, respectful, and enjoyable online environment. Remember, your actions online are as important as your actions in the real world. Always practice good cyber ethics!

The master software responsible for managing and coordinating all the operations of a computer is called Operating System (OS).

Functions Of An Operating System
1) Managing Hardware

The OS controls all the computer's hardware, like the keyboard, mouse, screen, and printer. It makes sure each part works correctly and talks to the other parts.
2) Running Applications

The OS allows you to open and use application software like word processors, web browsers, games, and more. It makes sure the applications have the resources they need, like memory and processing power.
3) File Management

The OS organizes all your files and folders. It helps you save, open, move, copy, and delete files. It also keeps track of where files are stored on your computer's hard drive.
4) User Interface

- The OS provides the user interface, which is what you see on the screen and how you interact with your computer.

- There are two main types of user interfaces:

1) Graphical User Interface (GUI)

This is what most people use. It includes windows, icons, buttons, and menus that you can click on with a mouse.



2) Command Line Interface (CLI)

This is where you type text commands to tell the computer what to do. It's mostly used by experts and programmers.
5) Multitasking

The OS allows you to run more than one program at the same time. For example, you can listen to music while browsing the internet and typing a document.
6) Security

The OS helps keep your computer safe from viruses and unauthorized users. It includes features like passwords, user accounts, and permissions to control who can access certain files or programs.

Computer Virus

Computer Virus Introduction
A computer virus is a type of harmful software designed to spread between computers, much like a biological virus spreads between people. It can damage data, disrupt software, and sometimes even affect the hardware, causing significant harm to computer systems.

How Do Computer Viruses Work?
A computer virus attaches itself to a legitimate program or file. When the program or file is opened, the virus is activated and can start spreading to other files, programs. Once a virus is activated, it can do many harmful things, like:
Deleting or Corrupting Files
It can erase important files or make them unusable.
Slowing Down The Computer
It can use up a lot of the computer's resources, making it slow and difficult to use.
Stealing Information
Some viruses are designed to steal personal information, like passwords or credit card numbers.
Causing The Computer To Crash
A virus can cause the computer to freeze or crash frequently.

Types of Computer Viruses
File Infector Virus
It attaches itself to executable files (programs you can run, like games or applications). When the infected program is opened, the virus spreads. For example "CIH" virus, which used to destroy data on computers.
Macro Virus
It Infects documents like those created in Microsoft Word or Excel. It spreads when the document is opened on another computer. For example "Melissa" virus, which spread through infected Word documents.
Boot Sector Virus
It Infects the part of the hard drive that starts up the computer (the boot sector). It activates when the computer is turned on. For example "Michelangelo" virus, which activated on March 6th every year.
Worms
Worms is like a virus but doesn't need to attach to a program. It spreads on its own by copying itself to other computers. For example "ILOVEYOU" virus, which spread through emails and caused widespread damage.
Trojan Horse
A Trojan horse looks like a normal program but actually contains a virus. When you run the program, the virus is released. For exampe "Zeus" Trojan virus, which was used to steal banking information.
Ransomware
It locks the files on your computer and demands a payment (ransom) to unlock them. For example "WannaCry" ransomware virus, which affected thousands of computers worldwide.

How Do Computer Viruses Spread?
Email Attachments
Opening an infected email attachment can activate a virus.
Downloading Files
Downloading software or files from untrustworthy websites can introduce viruses.
Infected USB Drives
Plugging in an infected USB drive can transfer a virus to your computer.
Visiting Infected Websites
Some websites are designed to automatically download viruses to your computer.

How to Protect Your Computer From Viruses
Install Antivirus Software
Antivirus software can detect and remove viruses from your computer. It scans your files and programs regularly to make sure they are safe. Examples: Norton, McAfee, Avast
Keep Your Software Updated
Regularly updating your operating system and other software ensures you have the latest security features to protect against new viruses.
Be Careful With Email Attachments
Don't open email attachments from people you don't know or if the attachment looks suspicious.
Download Software From Trusted Sources
Only download software from official websites or trusted app stores.
Use Strong Passwords
Strong passwords help protect your computer from unauthorized access.
Avoid Clicking On Pop-Up Ads
Pop-up ads can sometimes contain viruses. It's safer to close them immediately.

What to Do If Your Computer Gets a Virus
Run An Antivirus Scan
Use your antivirus software to scan your computer and remove the virus.
Disconnect From the Internet
This can prevent the virus from spreading to other computers or sending out your personal information.
Backup Your Files
Regularly back up your important files so you don't lose them if your computer gets infected.
Seek Professional Help
If the virus is serious, you may need to get help from a computer expert.

Computer Network

Computer Network
A computer network is a group of computers and other devices connected together to share resources like files, internet connections, and printers. Networks allow computers to communicate with each other quickly and easily.

Types of Computer Networks
Local Area Network (LAN)
A LAN connects computers within a small area like a home, school, or office.

Example: The computers in your school's computer lab might be connected to form a LAN

#Features Of Local Area Network (LAN):
- High-speed connection.
- Usually connects computers within a single building.
- Allows sharing of resources like printers and files.
Wide Area Network (WAN)
A WAN connects computers over a large area, like different cities or countries.

Example: The internet is the largest WAN in the world, connecting computers globally.

#Features Of Wide Area Network (WAN):
- Covers a large geographical area.
- Slower than a LAN because it covers more distance.
- Often uses telephone lines, fiber optics, or satellites for connections.
Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)
A MAN connects computers within a larger area than a LAN but smaller than a WAN, like a city or town.

Example: A network that connects all the computers in different buildings within a city's government offices.

#Features Of Metropolitan Area Network (MAN):
- Covers a city or large campus.
- Faster than a WAN but covers a larger area than a LAN.
Personal Area Network (PAN)
A PAN is a small network used for connecting devices around a single person.

Example: The connection between your smartphone, smartwatch, and wireless earbuds.

#Features Of Personal Area Network (PAN):
- Very small range (a few meters).
- Often used for connecting personal devices like phones, tablets, and laptops.

Components Of A Computer Network
Computers (Nodes)
The devices that are connected to the network, like desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones.
Server
A powerful computer that provides resources and services to other computers in the network, like file storage or internet access.
Router
A device that connects different networks together, often used to connect a LAN to the internet.
Switch
A device that connects multiple computers within a LAN and manages the flow of data between them.
Cables/Wireless Connections
Cables (like Ethernet cables) physically connect computers and devices within a network. Wireless connections (like Wi-Fi) use radio waves to connect devices without cables.
Modem
A device that connects your network to the internet. It translates data between the internet and your network.

Benefits Of Computer Networks
Sharing Resources
Multiple computers can share a single printer, internet connection, or storage device, saving money and making it easier to access resources.
Communication
Networks allow people to communicate easily through email, instant messaging, and video calls, even if they are far apart.
File Sharing
Files and documents can be easily shared between computers without using physical storage devices like USB drives.
Data Security
Networks can be secured with passwords and encryption, protecting data from unauthorized access.
Centralized Data Management
Important data can be stored on a central server, making it easier to manage, back up, and protect.

Types of Network Connections
There are two main ways computers connect in a network

Wired Connections
- Wired connections computers and devices are connected using physical cables (like Ethernet cables).

- Advantages: Reliable and fast.

- Disadvantages: Requires physical cables, which can be messy and limit mobility.



Wireless Connections
- Wireless connections computers and devices connect using wireless signals, like Wi-Fi or Bluetooth.

- Advantages: No need for cables; allows for mobility.

- Disadvantages: Can be slower and less secure than wired connections.

Network Security
Keeping a network secure is important to protect data and prevent unauthorized access. Here are some ways to secure a network
Firewalls
Software or hardware that blocks unauthorized access to a network.
Antivirus Software
Protects computers from malware and viruses.
Encryption
Scrambles data so that only authorized users can read it.
Strong Passwords
Using strong, unique passwords helps prevent unauthorized access.
